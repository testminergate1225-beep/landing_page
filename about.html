How CBT works

CBT focuses on identifying and changing unhelpful thinking patterns (often called cognitive distortions). Below are examples and what CBT does to help.

🔄 Typical unhelpful thoughts

  • “I always fail”
  • “Things will never get better”
  • “Everyone is judging me”

Then CBT helps you

  • Challenge those thoughts
  • Replace them with more balanced, realistic ones
  • Change behaviours that reinforce the problem

🛠️ Techniques used in CBT

Thought records (tracking negative thinking)
Cognitive restructuring (challenging beliefs)
Behavioural activation (increasing positive activities)
Exposure therapy (gradually facing fears)
Problem-solving skills

✅ What CBT helps with

  • Anxiety and panic attacks
  • Depression
  • Stress and burnout
  • Phobias
  • OCD (Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder)
  • PTSD (trauma-related issues)
  • Sleep problems

⏱️ What to expect

  • Usually short-term (e.g., 6–20 sessions)
  • Structured sessions with goals
  • Often includes homework exercises between sessions
  • Can be done in person or online
